## The One-Line Test

> **Is this role about building an analytics function — not running one — at a consumer-first startup that cares about AI?**

If the honest answer is no, close the tab.

## Green Zone — Apply With Energy

Modeled on BetterSleep (Head of Data) and Smallpdf (Head of Data Analytics) — both scored "insane fit" on qualification and ambition.

**The role must have ALL of these:**

- Owns the full analytics function — platform, people, strategy
- Manages 2–4 people now, or credible path within 6–9 months
- Explicitly mentions building AI tooling (NL querying, anomaly detection, LLM-assisted reporting, internal agents)
- Self-serve analytics as a goal — reducing ad-hoc requests, not generating them
- Direct partnership with Product, Growth, or CEO/CPO — not buried under CFO or deep in engineering
- Consumer product with real engagement data: subscriptions, retention, funnels, cohorts

**The company must look like this:**

| Criterion | Green Threshold |
|---|---|
| Stage | Series A–B. Pre-A only if founders are data-obsessed. |
| Domain | Consumer app: gaming, entertainment, wellness, productivity, social, creator economy. |
| Analytics team today | 1–3 people. You're building, not inheriting. |
| Stack signals | BigQuery / dbt / Mixpanel or Amplitude / Metabase / Firebase. |
| AI signal | JD mentions AI tooling explicitly — not just "familiarity with AI tools." |
| Title | Head of Data / Analytics Lead / Analytics Manager. Not "Senior Analyst." |
| Location | India-based or async-remote. |

## Red Zone — Do Not Apply

Each pattern below appeared in roles that scored poor. Recognize them in 30 seconds and move on.

### 1. Reporting role in manager clothing
*Example: Wholsum Foods "Manager – Data Analytics"*

When a JD lists 10+ unrelated technical skills (ML deployment + ETL + data scraping + cloud VMs + ERP + APIs), the hiring manager doesn't know what they want. You'll be setting up dashboards for 2 years.

**Rule:** 10+ unrelated skills in requirements = walk away.

### 2. Data science role in disguise
*Example: Paytm "Business Analyst Manager – User Growth"*

If requirements include predictive models, ML for segmentation, propensity modeling, Python with scikit-learn — it's a data science role. You are not a data scientist.

**Rule:** "Python proficiency for modeling" or "ML concepts" in *requirements* (not nice-to-haves) = skip.

### 3. Pure infrastructure role
*Example: Deliveroo "Senior Analytics Engineer"*

100% dbt + Snowflake + Looker. You're good at infra, but only when it's 20–30% of the role. Full-time analytics engineering = no stakeholder work, no insight generation, no business impact.

**Rule:** No business partnership component = wrong kind of technical work.

### 4. Wrong domain
Fintech and B2B SaaS aren't your world. You'll spend 6 months learning the business instead of building. Consumer retention, live events, subscription economics, engagement funnels — that's where you have edge.

**Rule:** No MAU/DAU, no subscription funnel, no real user engagement data = domain mismatch.

### 5. Senior Analyst title
*Example: Nykaa "Analyst III"*

You left Kiwi as analytics lead managing 5. An IC role is a step backward, not a foot in the door.

**Rule:** No roles titled Analyst, Senior Analyst, or Analyst III. Impact without ownership is not enough.

## Trap Zone — Looks Right, Isn't Right

These feel like good fits in the first 30 seconds. They aren't.

### Trap 1: Right title, nothing to build
*Example: WishLink "Product Analytics Lead"*

Right title, right team (3 analysts), right mandate. But the product is too small and the scope is limited. You'd be bored fast.

**Test:** What does winning look like in 12 months? If the answer is "cleaner dashboards and better A/B testing" — not enough.

### Trap 2: Right role, wrong level
*Example: Headout "Manager, Product Analytics"*

Strong ambition fit, but one level above you on experimentation rigor. The ideal fit is the Lead reporting to this Manager — but that role doesn't exist.

**Test:** Am I genuinely strong on 70%+ of core requirements? If experimentation depth or statistical rigor is a *core* ask and I'm weak there — skip, and find equivalent companies at a slightly earlier stage.

### Trap 3: Exciting AI angle, wrong qualification
*Example: Product Analyst 4 (AI Foundations)*

Tracking LLM agents, KPIs for AI systems, influencing AI roadmap — exciting. But required 7+ years fintech/SaaS and deep Python proficiency you don't have.

**Action:** File as "inspiration for what to target," not "apply anyway."

### Trap 4: Big brand, boring role
*Example: JioSaavn "Senior Manager Product Analytics"*

Streaming analytics at a well-known brand sounds appealing. But it's a reporting role in a product analytics function. No ownership of the analytics function. No building mandate. No AI angle.

**Test:** Strip the brand name. Would you still apply? If not, it's the brand talking, not the fit.

## Pre-Apply Checklist

Run every JD through this. If Part 1 isn't all YES, stop.

### Part 1: Hard Filters (all must be YES)
- [ ] Title is Head of Analytics / Analytics Lead / Analytics Manager or equivalent?
- [ ] Company is Series A–B?
- [ ] Domain is consumer-facing? (Gaming, entertainment, wellness, social, productivity, creator economy)
- [ ] JD mentions *building* or *evolving* the analytics function — not just running or reporting?
- [ ] Real user engagement product with MAU/DAU, retention, subscriptions, or funnel data?
- [ ] India-based or async-remote?

### Part 2: Qualification Check (be honest)
- [ ] I meet 70%+ of requirements without heavy rationalization?
- [ ] If experimentation / A/B testing is core — am I prepared to address the gap honestly?
- [ ] Is this a data science role in disguise? (ML, Python for modeling, statistical methods = data science)
- [ ] Is the AI mention real — or just "familiarity with AI tools for productivity"?

### Part 3: Ambition Check
- [ ] In 12 months, can I say "I built the analytics brain of this company"?
- [ ] Real team to build/lead — or IC role with a nice title?
- [ ] Analytics reports to CEO, CPO, or equivalent? (CFO = caution)
- [ ] Scope large enough to still find new problems in year 2?

### Part 4: Company Health
- [ ] Evidence of product-market fit? (Downloads, users, revenue)
- [ ] Company talks about its data stack specifically — not just "data-driven culture" vaguely?
- [ ] Role created because of growth — not because someone left?

## Target Archetypes

### 1. Head of Data at a Consumer Subscription App
*The BetterSleep archetype.*

Consumer app with millions of users moving from growth-at-any-cost to sustainable acquisition. Own the data platform, define subscription metrics (trial-to-paid, LTV, churn), run experiments, build self-serve tooling, manage a small team. AI tooling explicitly in scope.

**Why you win:** Kiwi gave you subscription analytics, real-time dashboards, event quality, and team building.

**Signals to look for:** BigQuery + dbt + Mixpanel/Firebase; team of 2–3 today; metrics-first culture; explicit AI/LLM mandate.

### 2. Head of Analytics at an AI-Curious Consumer Startup
*The Smallpdf archetype.*

Company with a functional product that wants to transform from reactive reporting to strategic, AI-augmented analytics. You're rebuilding the function, not inheriting it. Heavy on data culture change.

**Why you win:** Consulting breadth — you've seen 10+ analytics setups and know what good looks like.

**Signals to look for:** "Transform analytics from reactive to proactive" language; AI in analytics workflows; team of 1–2 to manage and grow.

### 3. Analytics Lead at a Gaming / Entertainment / Live Experience Platform
*The Kiwi archetype at a new company.*

Consumer entertainment with real-time engagement, live events, subscription mechanics. Product lives or dies on retention.

**Why you win:** You literally did this for 4+ years. Real-time dashboards, live event metrics, event quality frameworks — shipped all of it.

**Signals to look for:** Gaming, live events, short-form video, social entertainment, creator economy.

## Known Gaps — Own These

Don't hide them. Interviewers will find them.

- **Experimentation depth:** You've designed and analyzed A/B tests but haven't owned a rigorous experimentation platform end-to-end. Frame as: "I've done this at X scale, here's how I'd formalize it at yours."
- **Python for analysis:** Functional (pipelines, automation, data apps) but not deep analytics (pandas/statsmodels). Don't claim it as a strength.
- **Fintech/B2B SaaS domain:** You don't have it. Don't try to acquire it now.
- **MMP attribution (AppsFlyer/SKAN):** You know the concepts but haven't worked with MMPs hands-on. Study this before applying to UA-heavy roles.
